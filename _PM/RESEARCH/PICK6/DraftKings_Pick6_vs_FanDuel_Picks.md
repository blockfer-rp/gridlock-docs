## Strategic Rationale: Why Enter Late?

### DraftKings' Defensive Entry (December 2023)
1. **Market Protection:** Competitors PrizePicks, Underdog, and Betr were capturing DFS market share
2. **Peer-to-Peer Advantage:** Avoided regulatory scrutiny facing house-based models
3. **Customer Acquisition:** Access to non-sports betting states (34+ jurisdictions)
4. **Brand Defense:** Prevent erosion of "DFS leader" positioning

### FanDuel's Even Later Entry (August 2025)
1. **Competitive Pressure:** Could not cede market entirely to DraftKings and specialists
2. **Regulatory Clarity:** Peer-to-peer model gained acceptance after 2023-2024 regulatory fights
3. **Market Access:** Penetrate high-value non-betting states (Texas, Georgia, Minnesota)
4. **Revenue Diversification:** Offset sports betting saturation

### Historical Context
Both DraftKings and FanDuel initially *opposed* pick'em platforms, lobbying against PrizePicks and Underdog. Their 2023-2025 launches represent strategic reversals after:
- Pick'em market reached $1+ billion in entry fees
- Peer-to-peer model gained regulatory acceptance
- State-by-state crackdowns on house-based models created opportunity
